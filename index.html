Background Information

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

Answers For Investigation

Q1

For this test, the independent variable is whether or not the words and the colors match, i.e. if the conditions are congruent (color and word match) or incongruent (color and word doesn't match). The dependent variable is the time it takes to say the color out loud.

Q2

The Stroop test is a test which is designed to measure interference in response time of a task. For this project we are handling a two-sample paired/dependent dataset, i.e. each row in the dataset comes from one person, so the two columns of data are dependent. Furthermore we have a two-sample dataset, and we have no knowledge of the population parameters. Our choice of statistical test therefore falls on the one-tailed t-test - one-tailed because we wish to test if there is a significant increase in response time in the incongruent case. For this project, there are two cases, i.e. the congruent and the incongruent.

  • The null hypothesis is: There is no significant difference in the response times (congruent vs incongruent): $$H_0: \mu_c - \mu_i = 0$$ or $$H_0: \mu_D = 0 $$ where $$ \mu_D = \mu_c - \mu_i $$
  • The alternative hypothesis is: There is a significant increase in the response time for the incongruent case (the time to respond will increase): $$H_a: \mu_c - \mu_i < 0$$ or $$H_a: \mu_D < 0 $$

Q5

Now we want to perform the statistical test. First we'll use a t-table to find the one-tailed critical t for a $\alpha = 0.05$. Then we'll compute the t-statistic and if our t-statistic is lower than our critical t. We'll then see if this results in a statistical significance of the results which will allow us to reject the null.
